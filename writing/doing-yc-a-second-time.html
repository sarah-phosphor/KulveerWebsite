Doing Y Combinator a Second Time

I participated in Y Combinator twice — first in the winter 2007 program and again in summer 2011, with Tagstand, a mobile payments company. Here's why the second experience was better.

Y Combinator's Increased Legitimacy

I remember when coming from England I'd mention YC to a few friends in San Francisco and not many people knew what exactly it was. By 2011, the organization had established itself as a premier startup accelerator.

Improved Processes and Partner Support

The organization expanded its team and formalized operations. Partners like Garry Tan provided direct assistance with business fundamentals such as store design, enabling immediate revenue generation. Sam Altman facilitated valuable introductions, including connections to Verizon's leadership.

Strategic Partnerships and Events

YC hosted specialized conferences (iOS development, SEO) and negotiated discounts with major services including AWS, Dropbox, Rackspace, and Microsoft.

Expanded Founder Network

The network grew from 13 companies in 2007 to 64 in summer 2011, creating broader collaboration opportunities among founders facing similar challenges.

Additional Funding

The Start Fund provided an extra $150,000, extending runway and enabling more ambitious planning beyond immediate survival.

Larger Cohort Benefits

Despite reduced intimacy with 150+ founders attending Tuesday dinners, meaningful connections formed naturally, particularly intensifying during final demo day preparations.

Moving Back to SF and Doing Y Combinator Again

I've been back in San Francisco for a week and it's intense. My team launched a store, achieved our first sale, and met an NFC creator within the first week, with approximately 80 days until demo day.

I feel greater confidence in this second YC experience compared to 2007. I've learned that working hard isn't enough — it's crucial to be effective, which means working on the right thing. Early-stage obsessions with minor details, like debating back-button functionality at Auctomatic, have given way to a faster, iterative approach.

The most critical thing to get right is your founding team. I've known my cofounder Srini for four years; Omar is Srini's longtime friend from Stanford. This careful team selection reflects my belief that founder chemistry matters more than almost anything else.

The YC program has grown substantially since 2007 — from 13 to 63 companies. While the increased scale means less individual company familiarity, operations have become more organized, with dedicated office hours and additional partner support (including my former cofounder Harj).

When asked why I'd return to YC despite prior success, I cite product focus, Paul Graham's backing, peer support, and enhanced exposure as compelling reasons.

I've noticed cultural shifts in San Francisco, where Y Combinator itself is now widely recognized. However, I still struggle explaining NFC (near field communication) to people — we have a demo video showing information transferring from an NFC business card to a phone.

San Francisco's summer weather is colder than expected. And the taxis here are still impossible.
